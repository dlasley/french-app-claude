## Les Prépositions de Lieu

**sous • derrière • sur**

### Sous (Under)
*The last s is not pronounced*
- **Definition:** Something is located *under* something else.
- **Example:** Le chat est sous la table. (The cat is under the table.)

### Sur (On)
*The R is pronounced*
- **Definition:** Something is located *on top of* something.
- **Example:** Le livre est sur la table. (The book is on the table.)

### Derrière (Behind)
- **Definition:** Something is *behind* something else.
- **Example:** La voiture est derrière la maison. (The car is behind the house.)

## 1. How Negation Works (page 87) *Révision*

### To make a sentence negative:
- **ne + verb + pas** = Identify the verb and it's as simple as plugging the ne to the front and back of the verb.

**Examples:**
- Je parle français. → Je ne parle pas français.
- Il aime le sport. → Il n'aime pas le sport.

---

## 2. Ne → n' Before Vowels & H + Notes

When ne comes before a vowel or silent h, it becomes n'.

**Examples:**
- Elle habite à Paris. → Elle n'habite pas à Paris.
- J'aime le chocolat. → Je n'aime pas le chocolat.
- Ils écoutent la musique. → Ils n'écoutent pas la musique.

**Key Notes:**
- The apostrophe is only used before vowel sounds or silent h.
- The verb must always be inside ne / n' ... pas.

## How *avoir* Is Used in French (page 117)

In French, the verb **avoir** (to have) is used where English uses "to be."

- To say your **age**, French uses avoir:
  - J'ai 15 ans. → I am 15 years old.

- To say you are **hungry**, French also uses avoir:
  - J'ai faim. → I am hungry.

English uses "to be," but French uses "to have" for these expressions.

## Question avec est-ce que (page 147)

- **Est-ce que** is used to form yes/no questions.
- It does not translate directly, but it means: Do? Does? Is? Are?
